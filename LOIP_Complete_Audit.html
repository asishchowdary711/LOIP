SECTION 1

Executive Summary

Project Name

LOIP -- Loan Onboarding Intelligence Platform (also branded as "OnboardTrust AI" in the React frontend).

What Problem It Solves

LOIP automates the end-to-end personal loan onboarding process for the Indian market. It replaces manual, error-prone KYC document verification, income assessment, fraud detection, and credit decisioning with an AI-powered pipeline that processes identity documents (PAN, Aadhaar), income proofs (salary slips, bank statements, ITR, Form 16, GST returns), detects fraudulent or tampered documents, reconciles conflicting information across multiple sources, assesses borrower affordability, and produces an explainable approve/review/reject decision -- all within seconds rather than days.

Why It Was Created

Indian lending institutions face a convergence of pressures that make manual loan onboarding unsustainable:

  • Regulatory acceleration: RBI Digital Lending Guidelines (2022), DPDP Act 2023, Video KYC (V-CIP) circulars, and PMLA/AML requirements create an ever-growing compliance burden that manual processes struggle to satisfy consistently.
  • Fraud sophistication: Synthetic identities, digitally manipulated salary slips, PAN farming rings, and document forgery have outpaced human review capabilities. Manual reviewers miss subtle cross-document inconsistencies.
  • Customer experience demands: Borrowers expect instant or same-day loan decisions. Multi-day manual KYC with branch visits is a competitive disadvantage against fintech competitors.
  • Operational cost: Each manual underwriting decision involves 3-5 human touchpoints (document collection, KYC verification, income analysis, credit assessment, decisioning). At scale, this is prohibitively expensive.

SECTION 2

Problem Statement

2.1 Current State (As-Is): Manual Loan Origination in India

Personal loan origination at Indian banks and NBFCs is a labor-intensive, multi-day process involving multiple human touchpoints, fragmented document handling, and inconsistent quality controls. The typical workflow looks like this:

Step 1: Application Collection (Day 1)

The borrower submits a paper or digital form with personal details (name, PAN, Aadhaar, employment, income, desired loan amount) along with physical or scanned copies of KYC documents (PAN card, Aadhaar card), income proofs (salary slips, bank statements, Form 16/ITR), and employment verification (offer letter). Documents arrive in varying formats -- photographed at angles, poorly scanned, multi-page PDFs, WhatsApp-compressed images.

Step 2: KYC Document Verification (Day 1-2)

A KYC officer manually inspects each document: checks PAN format (XXXXX0000X), verifies Aadhaar number against UIDAI (often via manual OTP process), matches the applicant's name across PAN, Aadhaar, and the application form (accounting for spelling variants, initials, middle names). The officer visually inspects documents for signs of tampering (font inconsistencies, editing artifacts) and cross-checks date of birth and address across documents. This is error-prone: subtle mismatches (a transposed initial, a DOB off by one year) are frequently missed under workload pressure.

Step 3: Income Assessment (Day 2-3)

A credit analyst examines salary slips to extract gross pay, net pay, deductions (PF, TDS, PT). They manually compare salary slip figures against bank statement credits to verify the salary is real. If Form 16 or ITR is provided, they cross-reference annual income figures. For self-employed borrowers, the analyst must parse ITR-4 returns, GST filings, and bank statement cash flows to reconstruct income -- a highly subjective process. Income inflation (fabricated salary slips with inflated figures) is difficult to catch without systematic cross-source comparison.

Step 4: Credit Bureau Pull and Affordability Check (Day 2-3)

With borrower consent, the institution pulls a CIBIL/Experian/Equifax credit report. The analyst manually computes FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) by adding existing EMIs, credit card dues, and the proposed EMI, then dividing by verified monthly income. They assess whether the borrower can sustain the loan payments. This calculation depends entirely on the accuracy of the income figure from Step 3 -- if that figure was inflated, the FOIR is understated.

Step 5: Decision and Disbursement (Day 3-7)

A decision-maker (senior underwriter or credit committee) reviews the file, makes an approve/review/reject call, and documents the reasoning. If approved, KFS (Key Fact Statement) is generated, cooling-off period starts, NACH mandate is set up, and funds are disbursed to the borrower's verified bank account. For rejected or borderline cases, the file may cycle through multiple reviewers.

Identity Verification Risks

  • Name Mismatch: Indian names have high variability -- "Rajesh Kumar" on PAN vs "R. Kumar" on Aadhaar vs "Rajesh Kumar Singh" on application. Manual matching is inconsistent; one reviewer may accept a match another would reject.
  • Aadhaar Checksum: The 12-digit Aadhaar number has a Verhoeff checksum that prevents random number guessing. Manual entry errors go undetected if this checksum is not validated algorithmically.
  • Liveness/Spoofing: Without biometric liveness detection, a fraudster can present a printed photograph or screen image of someone else's face during video KYC.

Income and Affordability Risks

  • Income Inflation: The most common personal loan fraud. A borrower inflates salary slip figures while bank statement credits tell the real story. Without systematic cross-source reconciliation, the inflated figure is accepted.
  • Source Trust Asymmetry: Not all income documents are equally reliable. Form 16 (tax-deducted at source by employer) is harder to fabricate than a salary slip (which the employer may produce in-house with no centralized verification). Manual processes treat all sources equally.
  • Obligation Underreporting: Borrowers may not declare all existing EMIs. Without parsing bank statement debits for recurring fixed-amount outflows, existing obligations are understated and FOIR is artificially low.
  • Self-Employed Complexity: ITR-4 (presumptive income), GST returns, and bank statement cash flows must be reconciled to establish income for self-employed borrowers. This is highly subjective and analyst-dependent.

Compliance Risks

  • DPDP Act 2023: Processing Aadhaar, PAN, and financial data without explicit, recorded consent violates data principal rights. Manual processes often lack auditable consent records.
  • RBI Data Localization: Financial data stored on servers outside India violates RBI circular. Without system-enforced data residency checks, cloud misconfigurations can cause violations.
  • KFS Disclosure: RBI Digital Lending Guidelines 2022 require upfront disclosure of APR, processing fee, tenure, and EMI in a standardized Key Fact Statement. Manual generation is inconsistent.
  • V-CIP Requirement: Fully digital personal loans above a threshold amount require Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) per RBI January 2020 circular. Without system enforcement, this gate can be bypassed.
  • AML/PMLA: Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening and enhanced due diligence for high-value loans is mandated but inconsistently applied in manual workflows.

Customer Experience Issues

  • 2-7 Day Decision Time: Borrowers wait days for a decision that could be made in seconds with automation. During this wait, they may apply at competitors.
  • Opaque Process: Borrowers have no visibility into where their application stands, what stage it is in, or why it was rejected. No real-time status tracking.
  • Repeated Document Requests: When documents are lost, misclassified, or rejected, the borrower must re-upload, re-submit, and wait again.
  • Branch Dependency: Pre-V-CIP, physical branch visits were required for KYC. Even with V-CIP, many institutions have not implemented fully digital flows.

Operational Costs

  • Each manual underwriting decision involves 3-5 distinct human roles: document collector, KYC officer, income analyst, credit analyst, decision-maker.
  • At a cost of INR 500-2,000 per manual decision (personnel time, infrastructure, rework), a lender processing 10,000 applications/month spends INR 50L-2Cr/month on underwriting alone.
  • Rework rate on manually processed applications (document re-requests, re-reviews) is typically 20-30%, multiplying costs.

Business Requirements

3.1 Functional Requirements

FR-01: Document Intelligence

  • The system SHALL classify uploaded documents into one of 10 types (PAN, Aadhaar, salary_slip, bank_statement, form16, itr, gst_return, offer_letter, passport, driving_licence) using LayoutLMv3 with a confidence threshold of >= 0.85. Documents below this threshold SHALL be flagged for human review. (Source: loip/schemas/evidence.py:DocumentType, loip/domains/document_intel/processor.py)
  • The system SHALL perform dual-OCR extraction using PaddleOCR as primary and Surya as fallback when primary confidence is below 0.9. If both engines disagree by more than 0.15 in confidence, a conflict flag SHALL be raised. (Source: loip/models/paddleocr_wrapper.py, loip/models/surya_wrapper.py)
  • The system SHALL extract structured fields from documents using Qwen2.5-VL (via Ollama) as the primary extraction engine and Donut as fallback when Qwen confidence is below 0.7. (Source: loip/models/qwen2_5_vl_wrapper.py, loip/models/donut_wrapper.py)
  • The system SHALL support PDF document upload by converting PDF pages to images via PyMuPDF before OCR processing. (Source: loip/web/routes/demo.py)

FR-02: Identity Verification

  • The system SHALL validate PAN format against regex [A-Z]{5}[0-9]{4}[A-Z] and verify PAN status (active/inactive) via NSDL API. (Source: loip/validation.py, loip/integrations/nsdl_client.py)
  • The system SHALL validate Aadhaar numbers using the Verhoeff checksum algorithm (12 digits, not starting with 0 or 1, checksum valid). (Source: loip/validation.py:verhoeff_checksum_valid, loip/validation.py:is_valid_aadhaar)
  • The system SHALL verify Aadhaar via UIDAI OTP eKYC, subject to prior DPDP consent. (Source: loip/integrations/uidai_client.py)
  • The system SHALL perform cross-document entity matching using BGE-M3 semantic similarity with thresholds: name >= 0.85, PAN exact, DOB exact, address >= 0.70. (Source: loip/schemas/identity.py:EntityMatch)
  • The system SHALL perform face verification (ArcFace cosine similarity >= 0.60) and liveness detection (MiniFASNet score >= 0.50) using uploaded selfie vs document photo. (Source: loip/models/arcface_wrapper.py, loip/models/minifasnet_wrapper.py)
  • The system SHALL implement a webcam liveness challenge with 3 sequential steps: turn right (yaw < -18 degrees), turn left (yaw > +18 degrees), blink (EAR below threshold). (Source: loip/web/routes/demo.py)
  • The system SHALL decode QR codes from Aadhaar and PAN cards, verify Aadhaar Secure QR RSA-2048 signatures, and cross-check QR fields against OCR-extracted fields with similarity thresholds (name >= 0.80, address >= 0.70, DOB exact, UID last-4 exact). (Source: loip/domains/qr_trust/)
  • The system SHALL perform ELA (Error Level Analysis) and EXIF metadata inspection for document tampering detection. Both anomalies must fire to set QR_TAMPERED flag. (Source: loip/domains/qr_trust/tampering.py)
  • The system SHALL produce an IdentityVerificationResult with 13 defined identity flags (IdentityFlag enum), confidence score 0.0-1.0, and evidence chains. (Source: loip/schemas/identity.py)

FR-03: Income Intelligence

  • The system SHALL reconcile income from up to 5 sources (salary_slip, bank_statement, form16, itr, gst_return) using source-trust weighted averaging with configurable weights: Form 16 (0.90), ITR (0.85), bank credits (0.75), salary slip (0.65). (Source: loip/domains/income_intel/processor.py)
  • The system SHALL detect 11 income anomaly flags including salary_slip_vs_bank_mismatch (>30% difference), employer_name_mismatch, income_below_rbi_minimum, bank_credit_volatility (CV > 0.30), income_inflation, and no_salary_credit_found. (Source: loip/schemas/income.py:IncomeFlag)
  • The system SHALL support both salaried and self-employed segments with different income reconstruction logic. Self-employed uses ITR-4 (2-year average), GST turnover (25% margin assumption), and 12-month bank credits. (Source: loip/schemas/income.py:IncomeResult.segment)
  • The system SHALL detect salary credits in bank statements by matching patterns (narration keywords, amount consistency CV < 0.15, date recurrence +/- 5 days, frequency >= 4 of 6 months). (Source: build plan section 6.3)

FR-04: Affordability Assessment

  • The system SHALL compute FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) as total_obligations / verified_monthly_income where total_obligations = existing_obligations + proposed_emi. (Source: loip/schemas/affordability.py:AffordabilityResult.foir)
  • The system SHALL compute proposed EMI using the standard reducing-balance formula from loan amount, annual interest rate, and tenure in months. (Source: loip/domains/affordability/processor.py)
  • The system SHALL compute disposable income, liquidity score, cashflow stability, and financial stress score. (Source: loip/schemas/affordability.py)
  • The system SHALL detect 5 affordability anomaly flags: FOIR_EXCEEDED, FOIR_MARGINAL, CASHFLOW_UNSTABLE, FINANCIAL_STRESS_HIGH, DISPOSABLE_INCOME_INSUFFICIENT. (Source: loip/schemas/affordability.py:AffordabilityFlag)
  • The system SHALL produce a LightGBM affordability score (0.0-1.0). (Source: loip/models/lightgbm_wrapper.py)

FR-05: Credit Bureau Integration

  • The system SHALL pull credit bureau reports from CIBIL (primary) with Experian (fallback), subject to explicit borrower consent verification. (Source: loip/integrations/cibil_client.py, loip/integrations/experian_client.py)
  • The CreditBureauResult SHALL include: score (300-900), active_loans, overdue_accounts, dpd_90_plus (boolean), total_outstanding, enquiry_count_last_6m, and evidence chain. (Source: loip/schemas/bureau.py)

FR-06: Fraud Detection

  • The system SHALL detect 11 fraud signal types: DOCUMENT_FORGERY, SYNTHETIC_IDENTITY_RING, PAN_FARMING, EMPLOYEE_RECORD_FORGERY, ADDRESS_INCONSISTENCY_RING, EMPLOYER_SHELL, BEHAVIORAL_ANOMALY, INCOME_MANIPULATION, QR_SIGNATURE_INVALID, QR_DATA_MISMATCH, QR_TAMPERED. (Source: loip/schemas/fraud.py:FraudSignalType)
  • The system SHALL maintain a Neo4j identity graph with 8 node types (Person, PAN, Aadhaar, Phone, Email, Device, Employer, BankAccount, Address) and uniqueness constraints on each. (Source: loip/graph.py)
  • The system SHALL detect fraud rings via Cypher queries: PAN farming (shared phone/email to multiple PANs), synthetic identity rings (shared device to multiple persons), address inconsistency rings (shared address to >= 3 applications). (Source: loip/graph.py)
  • The system SHALL produce a GraphSAGE anomaly score. Fraud score > 0.80 SHALL trigger hard reject. (Source: loip/models/graphsage_wrapper.py, loip/domains/fraud/processor.py)

FR-07: Risk Decisioning

  • The system SHALL implement a 5-layer decision engine: (1) Hard KYC rejects, (2) Hard credit rejects (CIBIL < 650, DPD 90+, overdue accounts), (3) Hard affordability rejects (FOIR > 0.60, income below minimum), (4) Soft review triggers (8 conditions), (5) XGBoost ensemble scoring with 7 input features. (Source: loip/domains/risk_decisioning/processor.py)
  • The system SHALL produce a 3-tier decision: APPROVE (score >= 0.70), REVIEW (score 0.40-0.70), REJECT (score < 0.40). (Source: loip/schemas/decision.py:Decision)
  • The system SHALL block disbursal when V-CIP is required but not completed, even for otherwise-approvable loans. (Source: loip/schemas/decision.py:OnboardingDecision.disbursal_blocked)
  • The OnboardingDecision SHALL carry the complete sub-results (identity, income, affordability, bureau, fraud), reason codes (categorized by identity/credit/income/affordability/risk), review flags, SHAP risk factors, copilot narrative, and evidence chains. (Source: loip/schemas/decision.py)

FR-08: Human Review

  • The system SHALL automatically create review cases for all REVIEW and REJECT decisions. (Source: loip/pipelines/onboarding.py lines 176-178)
  • The system SHALL provide queue management: listing with filtering (status, assigned_to, risk score), pagination, sorting, summary statistics (pending, in-progress, completed, escalated, average age). (Source: loip/domains/human_review/processor.py)
  • The system SHALL support case assignment to specific reviewers, override workflow (approve/reject/escalate with mandatory reason code and optional notes), and override persistence. (Source: loip/schemas/db_models.py:ReviewOverrideRecord)
  • The system SHALL export retraining data from reviewer overrides (original features + override decision) for model improvement. (Source: loip/domains/human_review/processor.py)

21.5 Assumptions

  1. Scope is personal loans (unsecured consumer credit) in India only. Mortgage, vehicle loans, LAP, and business loans are out of scope.
  2. Loan parameters: INR 50,000 - 40,00,000 amount, 12-60 months tenure, 10.5-24% interest rate, minimum CIBIL score 700 (hard reject at 650).
  3. Employment tiers: 5 tiers from PSU/Government (lowest risk) to Startup/SME (highest risk), used as a feature in risk scoring.
  4. FOIR ceiling: 50% for salaried (pass), 51-60% (review), >60% (reject). This is the primary affordability metric per Indian lending practice.
  5. Minimum monthly income: INR 20,000 (lender-configurable floor).
  6. Source-trust hierarchy: Form 16 (0.90) > ITR (0.85) > bank statement (0.75) > salary slip (0.65). Tax-deducted-at-source documents are hardest to fabricate.
  7. API-authoritative reconciliation: UIDAI, NSDL, and CIBIL API responses automatically win over document-extracted values when there is a conflict.
  8. India data residency: All services deployed in India region. Cross-border transfer of financial data is prohibited.
  9. Both PAN and Aadhaar are mandatory per RBI KYC Master Direction for personal loans.
  10. V-CIP is required for fully digital personal loans without branch visit (configurable threshold).

End-to-End Workflow: Detailed Step-by-Step

The LOIP platform implements a complete loan origination pipeline from applicant onboarding through final decisioning. The system operates as a dual-stack architecture: a Python/FastAPI backend (loip/) implements the ML-heavy intelligence pipeline with domain-driven design, while a Node.js/Express backend (backend/) provides the customer-facing CRUD, authentication, and a parallel processing pipeline. Both backends share the same PostgreSQL database and can operate independently or together.

Step 1: Applicant Registration and Application Submission

Input: Applicant personal details (name, DOB, employer, declared income, loan amount, tenure), uploaded document images (PAN card, Aadhaar card, salary slip, bank statement, address proof, liveness video/selfie)

Processing:

  • Express backend (backend/src/controllers/loanController.ts): Accepts multipart form data via Multer middleware. Documents are encrypted at rest using AES-256-CBC (backend/src/utils/crypto.ts) before being written to disk. Application data is stored as JSONB in the loans table.
  • FastAPI backend (loip/web/routes/onboard.py): Accepts file uploads via python-multipart, stores raw images in memory (or MinIO via DocumentStore), and creates a LoanApplication Pydantic model (loip/schemas/decision.py).
  • Authentication uses JWT tokens with bcrypt password hashing. Demo users are seeded at startup.

Output: Application ID (UUID), stored loan record with status pending, encrypted document files on disk (Express) or MinIO buckets (FastAPI)

Technologies: Express.js, Multer, bcryptjs, jsonwebtoken, AES-256-CBC encryption, PostgreSQL, FastAPI, python-multipart, MinIO

Models Used: None at this stage

Step 2: Document Classification

Input: Raw document images (PNG/JPEG/PDF)

Processing:

  • FastAPI pipeline (loip/domains/document_intel/processor.py): Uses LayoutLMv3 (loip/models/layoutlmv3_wrapper.py) for document classification. The classifier identifies document type from a set of 6 classes: Aadhaar, PAN, Salary Slip, Bank Statement, Form16, ITR. If LayoutLMv3 confidence is below 0.85, it falls back to Qwen2.5-VL via Ollama for vision-based classification. Mock mode returns deterministic classes based on image height.
  • Express pipeline (backend/src/engines/classifier.ts): Uses filename-based heuristic classification (keyword matching in basename) to determine document type. Returns confidence scores between 50-99%.

Output: ClassificationResult with document class, confidence score, logits per class, and needs_review flag

Technologies: LayoutLMv3 (HuggingFace Transformers), Qwen2.5-VL (via Ollama), OpenCV

Models Used:

  • LayoutLMv3 (microsoft/layoutlmv3-base, fine-tuned checkpoint at loip/models/checkpoints/layoutlmv3-finetuned/)
  • Qwen2.5-VL 3B (Qwen/Qwen2.5-VL-3B-Instruct via Ollama qwen2.5vl:3b)

Step 3: OCR (Optical Character Recognition)

Input: Classified document images

Processing:

  • FastAPI pipeline (loip/domains/document_intel/processor.py): Primary OCR via PaddleOCR (loip/models/paddleocr_wrapper.py). If mean confidence falls below the fallback threshold, secondary OCR via Surya (loip/models/surya_wrapper.py) is invoked. If the two engines disagree by more than the conflict threshold, an OCRConflict is returned for human review. Returns bounding boxes, text, and confidence per region.
  • Express pipeline (backend/src/engines/ocrEngine.ts): Decrypts the file, writes to a temp file, calls Python ocr_bridge.py which uses MarkItDown for OCR. Falls back to mock OCR with structured data generation if Python dependencies are unavailable. Regex-based field extraction from Markdown output (Aadhaar number pattern, PAN pattern, DOB).

Output: OCRResult with boxes (text, confidence, bbox), mean confidence, engine name, raw text. Or OCRConflict with primary/secondary results and delta.

Technologies: PaddleOCR, Surya OCR, MarkItDown, OpenCV, PyMuPDF

Models Used:

  • PaddleOCR (angle classification + detection + recognition, lang=en)
  • Surya OCR (surya-ocr >= 0.6)

Step 4: Field Extraction

Input: Document images + document class

Processing:

  • FastAPI pipeline: Primary extraction via Qwen2.5-VL (loip/models/qwen2_5_vl_wrapper.py). The model receives the image with a structured prompt requesting JSON with specific field names (defined in FIELD_SPECS per document class). Supports three backends: Ollama (preferred, sends base64 image via /api/generate), HuggingFace Transformers (full model weights), and mock mode. If primary confidence is below the extraction fallback threshold, Donut (loip/models/donut_wrapper.py) is used as secondary extractor.
  • PAN fields: pan_number, full_name, date_of_birth
  • Aadhaar fields: aadhaar_number
  • Salary Slip fields: employer_name, net_pay
  • ITR fields: total_income
  • GST Return fields: turnover_b2b, turnover_b2c
  • Express pipeline: Field extraction is integrated into the OCR engine via regex matching and mock data generation with applicant-specific values.

Output: ExtractionResult with list of ExtractionField (name, value, confidence), model name, overall confidence

Technologies: Qwen2.5-VL (Ollama/HuggingFace), Donut, OpenCV

Models Used:

  • Qwen2.5-VL 3B Instruct (primary extractor)
  • Donut (naver-clova-ix/donut-base, secondary extractor)

Step 5: QR Trust Verification

Input: Document images of QR-eligible classes (PAN, Aadhaar)

Processing: (loip/domains/qr_trust/processor.py, loip/domains/qr_trust/aadhaar_qr.py, loip/domains/qr_trust/pan_qr.py, loip/domains/qr_trust/tampering.py)

  • Detects and decodes QR codes using pyzbar
  • For Aadhaar: parses UIDAI Secure QR XML payload (loip/validation.py), verifies RSA-2048 digital signature against UIDAI public key, cross-references QR data (name, DOB, UID) against OCR-extracted fields using configurable similarity thresholds (name >= 0.80, address >= 0.70)
  • For PAN: validates PAN format ([A-Z]{5}[0-9]{4}[A-Z])
  • Tampering detection: Error Level Analysis (ELA) on image regions around QR, EXIF metadata analysis for editing software signatures
  • Produces trust flags: QR_SIGNATURE_INVALID, QR_NAME_MISMATCH, QR_DOB_MISMATCH, QR_ID_NUMBER_MISMATCH, QR_TAMPERED

Output: QRTrustResult with trust_score, flags, per-document decode results

Technologies: pyzbar, cryptography (RSA verification), defusedxml, Pillow, OpenCV

Models Used: None (deterministic cryptographic verification)

Step 6: Identity Verification

Input: Extracted fields from PAN/Aadhaar/Passport, application data, selfie image, document face image, QR results

Processing: (loip/domains/identity_trust/processor.py and backend/src/engines/identity.ts)

  • API Verification:
  • NSDL PAN verification (loip/integrations/nsdl_client.py): Verifies PAN against NSDL registry (number, name, DOB match)
  • UIDAI Aadhaar OTP verification (loip/integrations/uidai_client.py): Validates Aadhaar via OTP flow
  • Aadhaar Verhoeff checksum (loip/validation.py): Validates 12-digit Aadhaar with Verhoeff algorithm (dihedral group D5)
  • Face Verification:
  • ArcFace (loip/models/arcface_wrapper.py): Cosine similarity between selfie embedding and document face embedding. Uses InsightFace buffalo_l model. Threshold >= 0.60 for pass.
  • MiniFASNet liveness detection (loip/models/minifasnet_wrapper.py): Anti-spoofing via InsightFace. Checks pose angles (yaw < 30, pitch < 20), eye-aspect-ratio (> 0.15), detection score. Threshold >= 0.50.
  • Name Cross-Matching:
  • BGE-M3 embeddings (loip/models/bge_m3_wrapper.py): Semantic similarity between PAN name, Aadhaar name, and application name using BAAI/bge-m3 sentence transformer. Threshold >= 0.85.
  • Express pipeline: Levenshtein distance-based name similarity between Aadhaar and PAN names. Format validation (12-digit Aadhaar regex, PAN regex). DOB cross-checking across documents. Marriage name change detection (first name match + last name difference).
  • Document Metadata Tamper Check: Checks PDF producer/creator for Photoshop signatures
  • QR Trust Integration: Incorporates QR verification flags into identity confidence

Output: IdentityVerificationResult with identity_confidence (0.0-1.0), pan_verified, aadhaar_verified, face_verified, liveness_verified, entity_matches, tamper_flags (enum: NAME_PAN_AADHAAR_MISMATCH, DOB_MISMATCH, PAN_FORMAT_INVALID, AADHAAR_FORMAT_INVALID, SPOOF_DETECTED, FACE_MISMATCH, QR_SIGNATURE_INVALID, QR_DATA_MISMATCH, QR_TAMPERED, etc.), evidence_chains

Technologies: InsightFace (ArcFace + buffalo_l), sentence-transformers (BGE-M3), NSDL API, UIDAI API, Verhoeff algorithm

Models Used:

  • ArcFace (InsightFace buffalo_l, CPUExecutionProvider)
  • MiniFASNet / buffalo_l liveness heuristic
  • BGE-M3 (BAAI/bge-m3 sentence transformer)

Step 7: Fraud Detection

Input: Identity result, extracted data, application data, identity graph (Neo4j)

Processing: (loip/domains/fraud/processor.py and backend/src/engines/fraud.ts)

  • Document Forgery Rules:
  • Liveness detection failure triggers DOCUMENT_FORGERY signal
  • Passport MRZ checksum validation (ICAO Doc 9303 TD3 format) -- validates composite check digits
  • Document metadata tampering (Photoshop/GIMP/AI signatures in EXIF/XMP)
  • Neo4j Identity Graph Fraud Rings (loip/graph.py):
  • Ingests application into graph: Person node linked to PAN, Aadhaar, Phone, Email, Device, Employer, BankAccount, Address nodes
  • pan_farming: One phone/email shared across multiple distinct PANs (severity 0.6 + 0.1 * distinct_pans)
  • synthetic_identity_ring: One device shared across multiple persons (severity 0.7 + 0.1 * peers)
  • address_inconsistency_ring: One address shared by >= 3 applications (severity 0.5 + 0.1 * peers)
  • employer_shell: (defined in schema, shell company detection)
  • GraphSAGE Anomaly (loip/models/graphsage_wrapper.py): In-memory graph maintaining application connections via shared PAN/Aadhaar values. Features: pan_match_count, aadhaar_match_count, total_degree. Trained sklearn model (joblib checkpoint). Score > 0.8 triggers SYNTHETIC_IDENTITY_RING signal.
  • Express pipeline: Rule-based fraud detection combining identity score, income score, bank score, mismatch flags, metadata tampering alerts. Produces flags: IDENTITY_SUSPICIOUS, DOCUMENT_METADATA_TAMPERED, DOCUMENT_TAMPERING_SUSPECTED, TEMPORARY_SALARY_PARKING_INDICATOR, FAKE_EMPLOYER_PROFILING, DEVICE_FINGERPRINT_REUSE.
  • Gemini/OpenRouter Visual AI (backend/src/utils/geminiVision.ts): Sends base64 document image to Gemini 2.5 Flash (or via OpenRouter) with detailed multi-point fraud analysis prompt. Returns structured JSON fraud report with tampering detection, security feature verification, and QR code analysis.

Output: FraudResult with fraud_score (0.0-1.0), list of FraudSignal (signal_type, severity, description, evidence), graph_fraud_score, evidence_chains. Express: FraudResult with trust score (0-100), fraud flags, document integrity score.

Technologies: Neo4j (Cypher), GraphSAGE (sklearn), InsightFace, ICAO 9303 MRZ validation, Google Gemini 2.5 Flash API, OpenRouter API

Models Used:

  • GraphSAGE (sklearn, checkpoint graphsage_fraud.joblib)
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash (multimodal vision, via API)

Step 8: Income Analysis

Input: Extracted data from salary slip, bank statement, ITR, GST returns; application employer name; document IDs from MinIO

Processing: (loip/domains/income_intel/processor.py and backend/src/engines/income.ts)

  • Multi-Source Income Reconciliation with source-trust weighting:
  • ITR (current FY): weight 0.85 (salaried) / 0.90 (self-employed) -- highest trust
  • ITR (prior FY): weight 0.80 (for 2-year averaging)
  • GST Returns: weight 0.75, net income = turnover * 25% profit margin
  • Salary Slip: weight 0.65, parsed net_pay
  • Bank Statement: weight 0.75 (salaried) / 0.65 (self-employed), average of salary credits
  • Weighted Average: reconciled_annual = sum(amount * weight) / sum(weights)
  • Anomaly Detection:
  • SALARY_SLIP_VS_BANK_MISMATCH: > 30% deviation between salary slip and bank credits
  • INCOME_INFLATION: salary slip higher than bank credits
  • EMPLOYER_NAME_MISMATCH: extracted employer vs. declared employer
  • NO_SALARY_CREDIT_FOUND: salaried applicant with no matching bank credits
  • INCOME_BELOW_RBI_MINIMUM: verified monthly income < INR 20,000
  • SALARY_MISSING_MANDATORY_FIELDS
  • ML Confidence Scoring: XGBoost model predicts income confidence from features (salary_slip_amount, bank_credit_amount, anomalies count)
  • Express pipeline: Employer name fuzzy matching (word-level containment), salary amount deviation check (> 20%), deduction structure validation (net > gross = highly suspicious)
  • Evidence Chains: Each income source produces an EvidenceChain linking back to MinIO document IDs with reconciliation method and confidence

Output: IncomeResult with verified_monthly_income, reconciled_annual_income, income_confidence, income_sources (with trust weights), salary_credits, anomaly_flags, evidence_chains

Technologies: XGBoost, Pydantic, MinIO (document traceability)

Models Used:

  • XGBoost income confidence model (checkpoint xgboost_income_confidence.json, features: salary_slip_amount, bank_credit_amount, anomalies)

Step 9: Affordability Analysis

Input: Income result, application data (loan amount, tenure, interest rate), extracted bank statement data

Processing: (loip/domains/affordability/processor.py and backend/src/engines/affordability.ts)

  • EMI Computation: Standard reducing-balance formula: EMI = P * r * (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1) where r = annual_rate/12/100
  • FOIR Calculation: Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio = (existing_obligations + proposed_EMI) / verified_monthly_income
  • Disposable Income: verified_monthly_income - total_obligations - estimated_monthly_expenses (INR 15,000 configurable)
  • Express DTI: debt-to-income = existing_EMIs / monthly_income * 100. Risk bands: < 30% safe, 30-50% medium, > 50% high risk. Disposable income buffer check (< INR 15,000 warning).
  • ML Scoring: LightGBM predicts affordability score from features (foir, disposable_income, liquidity_score)
  • Anomaly Flags: FOIR_EXCEEDED (> 0.60), FOIR_MARGINAL (> 0.50), DISPOSABLE_INCOME_INSUFFICIENT (< 0)

Output: AffordabilityResult with foir, dti, disposable_income, proposed_emi, existing_obligations, liquidity_score, cashflow_stability, financial_stress_score, affordability_score, affordability_confidence, anomaly_flags, evidence_chains

Technologies: LightGBM, mathematical computation

Models Used:

  • LightGBM affordability model (checkpoint lightgbm_affordability.txt, features: foir, disposable_income, liquidity_score)

Step 10: Credit Bureau Check

Input: PAN number, DOB, full name, application ID, consent verification flag

Processing: (loip/integrations/cibil_client.py and backend/src/engines/bureau.ts)

  • CIBIL TransUnion API: Posts PAN + DOB + name to CIBIL API. Consent must be pre-verified (DPDP Act compliance). Returns credit score (300-900), active loans count, overdue accounts, DPD 90+ flag, total outstanding, enquiry count (last 6 months).
  • Express pipeline: Simulates or uses custom sandbox override values. Evaluates CIBIL score in bands: >= 800 (100 trust), >= 750 (85), >= 700 (70), >= 650 (55), >= 550 (30), < 550 (10 trust). Additional checks: credit utilization > 70% (-25), active loans > 4 (-15), recent enquiries > 4 (-15), repayment defaults (-40).
  • Hard gate: CIBIL score < 650 in FastAPI pipeline = auto-reject. Score < 550 in Express pipeline = auto-reject.

Output: CreditBureauResult with score, active_loans, overdue_accounts, dpd_90_plus, total_outstanding, enquiry_count, evidence chain

Technologies: CIBIL TransUnion API, httpx (async HTTP), PostgreSQL (credit_profiles table)

Models Used: None (API data + rule-based scoring)

Step 11: V-CIP (Video-based Customer Identification Process)

Input: Application ID, loan amount, consent status, agent ID

Processing: (loip/domains/identity_trust/vcip.py)

  • RBI January 2020 Circular compliance: Required for loans above INR 2,00,000 (configurable)
  • Session Lifecycle: initiate -> capture_geotag -> present_ovd -> answer_questions -> store_recording -> complete
  • Gates checked at completion:
  1. Session not expired (30-minute TTL)
  2. Consent verified
  3. Geotag within India (lat 6.5-37.6, lon 68.1-97.5 bounding box)
  4. OVD (Officially Valid Document) presented on camera
  5. Live face match against OVD photo (ArcFace >= 0.60)
  6. Liveness check (MiniFASNet >= 0.50)
  7. Random questions answered correctly (minimum 2 from pool of 8)
  8. Call recording stored to India-region storage
  • Flags: CONSENT_MISSING, GEOTAG_MISSING, GEOTAG_OUTSIDE_INDIA, OVD_NOT_PRESENTED, LIVENESS_FAILED, FACE_MISMATCH, QUESTIONS_FAILED, RECORDING_NOT_STORED, SESSION_EXPIRED

Output: VCIPResult with status (COMPLETED/FAILED), passed boolean, flags, recording_object_id

Technologies: ArcFace, MiniFASNet, MinIO (recording storage), WebRTC (planned)

Models Used: ArcFace (face match), MiniFASNet/buffalo_l (liveness)

Step 12: Risk Scoring

Input: All verification results (identity confidence, income confidence, FOIR, CIBIL score, cashflow stability, employment tier, loan-to-income ratio)

Processing: (loip/domains/risk_decisioning/processor.py and backend/src/engines/risk.ts)

  • FastAPI pipeline: XGBoost ensemble model predicts a risk score (0.0-1.0) from 7 features:
  • identity_confidence, income_confidence, foir, cibil_score_normalized ((score-300)/600), cashflow_stability, employment_tier, loan_to_income_ratio (loan_amount / (monthly_income * 12))
  • Express pipeline: Weighted aggregation of 6 sub-scores: identity (20%), income (20%), bank (15%), fraud (20%), credit (15%), affordability (10%). Final score 0-100, categorized: >= 71 Low Risk, 31-70 Medium Risk, <= 30 High Risk.

Output: Risk score (float 0.0-1.0 or integer 0-100), risk category

Technologies: XGBoost

Models Used:

  • XGBoost risk ensemble (checkpoint xgboost_risk_score.json, features: 7 normalized features)

Step 13: Explainability (SHAP + LIME + Copilot)

Input: Risk features, case data (application + identity + income + affordability + bureau + decision)

Processing: (loip/domains/explainability/processor.py)

  • SHAP (loip/domains/explainability/shap_explainer.py): TreeExplainer on XGBoost risk model. Produces per-feature SHAP values showing contribution direction and magnitude. Identifies top 3 positive and negative contributors. Feature display names: "Identity Confidence", "FOIR (Obligation Ratio)", "CIBIL Score", "Cashflow Stability", "Employment Risk Tier", "Loan-to-Income Ratio", etc.
  • LIME (loip/domains/explainability/lime_explainer.py): Explains individual field extractions -- why the model extracted a particular value from a particular region of the document.
  • Reviewer Copilot (loip/domains/explainability/copilot.py): Qwen3-32B LLM generates human-readable case narratives. Called via OpenAI-compatible API (vLLM serving). Prompt template includes full case context. Returns JSON with: profile_summary (3 sentences), primary_decision_reason, inconsistencies (up to 3 with source documents), reviewer_questions (2 specific questions). Falls back to mock narrative if LLM unavailable.

Output: ExplainabilityResult with shap_explanations, lime_explanations, copilot (CopilotNarrative), risk_factors (human-readable strings)

Technologies: SHAP, LIME, Qwen3-32B (via vLLM), httpx

Models Used:

  • SHAP TreeExplainer (on XGBoost risk model)
  • LIME
  • Qwen3-32B (reviewer copilot, via vLLM OpenAI-compatible endpoint)

Step 14: Decision Engine

Input: LoanApplication, IdentityVerificationResult, IncomeResult, AffordabilityResult, CreditBureauResult, FraudResult, VCIPResult

Processing: (loip/domains/risk_decisioning/processor.py and backend/src/engines/decision.ts)

Hard Reject Rules (FastAPI pipeline, checked in order):

  1. Fraud score > 0.80
  2. V-CIP session FAILED
  3. Identity confidence < 0.30
  4. PAN inactive (NSDL)
  5. Aadhaar OTP failed
  6. PAN/Aadhaar format invalid
  7. Name mismatch (PAN vs Aadhaar)
  8. DOB mismatch
  9. CIBIL score < 650
  10. DPD 90+ in last 24 months
  11. Overdue accounts in bureau
  12. No salary credit found (salaried segment)
  13. FOIR > 0.60
  14. Income below minimum requirement

Review Triggers:

  • Identity confidence < 0.70
  • Document metadata anomaly
  • FOIR > 0.50 (marginal)
  • Income anomaly flags present
  • Affordability anomaly flags present
  • CIBIL score < 700
  • Active loans > 3
  • Employment tier >= 4 (mid-size/startup)
  • V-CIP required but not completed

ML-Based Decision (if no hard rules triggered):

  • Risk score >= 0.70: APPROVE (unless V-CIP pending, then REVIEW)
  • Risk score 0.40-0.70: REVIEW
  • Risk score < 0.40: REJECT

Express pipeline decision logic:

  • REJECT: fraud detected OR KYC failed OR risk score <= 30 OR CIBIL < 550
  • MANUAL_REVIEW: minor mismatches OR medium risk (31-70) OR credit doubt (CIBIL < 700) OR affordability warnings
  • APPROVE: all checks pass, excellent risk profile

Output: OnboardingDecision with decision (APPROVE/REVIEW/REJECT), reason_codes, review_flags, risk_score, disbursal_blocked, disbursal_block_reason, identity/income/affordability/bureau/fraud results, evidence_chains, risk_factors, copilot_narrative, decided_at timestamp. Express: DecisionResult with recommendation, reasons array, risk breakdown.

Technologies: XGBoost ensemble, rule engine, Pydantic

Models Used: XGBoost risk score ensemble

Step 15: Human Review and Override

Input: OnboardingDecision for REVIEW/REJECT cases

Processing: (loip/domains/human_review/processor.py)

  • Creates ReviewCase with case_id, application_id, system_decision, risk_score, FOIR, CIBIL score, primary reason code, review flags
  • Queue management: filterable by status (PENDING/IN_PROGRESS/COMPLETED/ESCALATED), assignee, minimum risk score. Sortable by risk_score, created_at, loan_amount. Paginated.
  • Override workflow: reviewer can APPROVE, REJECT, or ESCALATE with reason codes: ADDITIONAL_DOCS_VERIFIED, EMPLOYER_CONFIRMED_DIRECTLY, INCOME_JUSTIFIED_BY_ALTERNATE_SOURCE, POLICY_EXCEPTION_APPROVED, MANUAL_FRAUD_CLEARED, FRAUD_CONFIRMED, INCOME_UNVERIFIABLE, IDENTITY_UNRESOLVABLE, SENIOR_REVIEW_NEEDED, COMPLIANCE_CONCERN
  • Feature snapshot captured at override time for retraining data
  • Overrides persisted to PostgreSQL review_overrides table with audit trail

Output: ReviewCase with updated status, OverrideRecord with full audit trail, retraining data export

Technologies: PostgreSQL, FastAPI, Pydantic

Models Used: None (human-in-the-loop)

Step 16: Compliance Checks

Input: Application data, consent records, loan terms

Processing: (loip/domains/compliance/processor.py)

  • DPDP Act 2023 Consent Management: Record, verify, withdraw consent per purpose. Data deletion with tombstone audit trail.
  • PMLA/AML: PEP (Politically Exposed Person) screening. AML risk assessment: high-value threshold INR 50,00,000, SAR flagging for fraud_score > 0.80, enhanced due diligence for high-value or PEP matches.
  • RBI Digital Lending Guidelines: Key Fact Statement (KFS) generation with APR calculation, EMI schedule, processing fees. KFS disclosure and acceptance workflow. Cooling-off period (3 days post-disbursal with cancellation option).
  • NACH Mandate: e-mandate for EMI auto-debit
  • PII Masking: Regex-based (mock) or Presidio-based (production) masking of PAN, Aadhaar, phone, email in text. Custom recognizers for Indian document numbers.
  • Data Residency: Validates all service endpoints are in India regions (ap-south, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Chennai, localhost patterns)

Output: Consent records, KFS documents, AML check results, PII-masked text, data residency validation

Technologies: Presidio (analyzer + anonymizer), regex, Pydantic

Models Used: Presidio NER models (for PII detection in production mode)

  5. consent.captured -- bureau consent + CIBIL score
  6. fraud.scored -- fraud score

7.1.1 MIDV-500 & MIDV-2020

What it is: MIDV-500 (Mid-level Video Document dataset, 500 variants) and MIDV-2020 (its extended successor) are public research datasets from Smart Engines, distributed via FTP at ftp.smartengines.com. They contain images and video frames of identity documents (passports, ID cards, driver licenses) from European and Russian jurisdictions, captured under various conditions (table surfaces, hand-held, keyboard backgrounds, monitor reflections, cluttered backgrounds). MIDV-500 contains approximately 50+ per-document-type ZIP archives totaling roughly 32 GB; MIDV-2020 extends this with additional lighting and angle variations (approximately 4.5 GB in its compressed form).

Why used: The build plan designates these datasets exclusively for OCR robustness testing -- validating that the OCR pipeline (PaddleOCR) can handle degraded capture conditions such as poor lighting, skewed angles, partial occlusions, and motion blur. They are explicitly NOT used for Indian document training. The download_datasets.py script includes a _generate_midv_report() function that analyzes the dataset distribution by document nationality/type and capture conditions, and generates a distribution_report.json with a prominent warning: "European/Russian ID corpus -- NOT suitable for Indian document training."

How it helps: If downloaded and used, MIDV-500/2020 would stress-test the OCR pipeline's ability to extract text from documents photographed in non-ideal conditions -- the same conditions end users would encounter when photographing their PAN cards or Aadhaar cards with a smartphone. The varied capture conditions (5 categories: table, hand, keyboard, monitor, clutter) provide coverage for real-world document submission quality.

Limitations:

  • Domain mismatch: European/Russian documents have different layouts, fonts, scripts, and field structures than Indian KYC documents (PAN, Aadhaar, salary slips, bank statements). The dataset cannot be used for training field extraction or document classification models.
  • Download status: DEFERRED INDEFINITELY. The URLs in the download script (https://ftp.smartengines.com/midv-500/dataset/midv-500.zip) return HTTP 404. The real datasets are distributed as 50+ per-document-type ZIP files via FTP, requiring a script rewrite.
  • Size constraint: At 32+ GB for MIDV-500 alone, it exceeds the project's 500 MB - 1 GB local data budget and the development machine's available disk space (~37 GB free at the time).
  • Lowest priority: Classified as lowest-priority in the build plan, deferred with "Phase B OCR models."
  • No checksum verification: sha256 is set to None in the DatasetSpec definition.

7.1.2 RVL-CDIP

Purpose: RVL-CDIP (Ryerson Vision Lab -- Complex Document Information Processing) is a large-scale document classification dataset containing approximately 400,000 grayscale document images across 16 categories: letter, form, email, handwritten, advertisement, scientific report, scientific publication, specification, file folder, news article, budget, invoice, presentation, questionnaire, resume, and memo.

Training use: The dataset's role is LayoutLMv3 backbone fine-tuning -- teaching the LayoutLMv3 model generic document layout understanding and visual feature extraction. The 16-category classification task forces the model to learn general document structure recognition (headers, tables, text blocks, signatures, logos) that transfers to Indian KYC document understanding. This is pre-training on generic layout features, NOT training an Indian document classifier.

Document classification role: RVL-CDIP provides the foundation for the LayoutLMv3 model's ability to distinguish between different document structures. When the model later sees an Indian PAN card versus a bank statement, the generic layout features learned from RVL-CDIP (understanding of text placement, table structures, header/footer patterns) help it classify and extract fields more accurately.

Current status: A partial subset of 320 images (20 per class, class-balanced, 38.6 MB) was downloaded via scripts/download_rvlcdip_subset.py. Label mappings come from data/train.txt (13.7 MB, downloaded in full). The script enforces safety budgets: --max-mb (default 100 MB) and --max-seconds (default 600s).

Limitations:

  • The full 38.7 GB archive is not gated but exceeds local disk.

7.1.3 FUNSD

Purpose: FUNSD (Form Understanding in Noisy Scanned Documents) is a dataset of 199 annotated English scanned forms with spatial annotations for form key-value structure understanding. It is approximately 17 MB (30 MB compressed).

Key-value extraction: Each FUNSD document is annotated with spatial bounding boxes and semantic labels that identify form elements as headers, questions, answers, or other structural components. The annotations include linked relationships between questions and their corresponding answers -- exactly the kind of structure the LOIP system needs to extract from Indian financial documents (e.g., "Employee PAN:" followed by "ABCDE1234F").

Form understanding: FUNSD serves as the Donut backbone pre-training dataset. The Donut model (Document Understanding Transformer) learns to parse form structures from FUNSD's annotations, developing an understanding of how form fields are spatially organized and how labels relate to values. This transfers to Indian forms like salary slips and Form 16 where similar key-value layouts exist.

Current status: DOWNLOADED SUCCESSFULLY at data/funsd/ (16.8 MB). It is the only external dataset that was fully downloaded. Fetched via scripts/download_datasets.py with checksum verification (checksum_verified: true in manifest.json).

Limitations:

  • Only 199 documents -- a small dataset that provides foundational but not exhaustive coverage.
  • English-only forms, no Hindi/regional language content (relevant for Aadhaar cards which have bilingual text).
  • Noisy scanned documents may not perfectly represent the clean synthetic PDFs generated by the LOIP pipeline.

7.1.4 DocVQA

Purpose: DocVQA (Document Visual Question Answering) is a benchmark dataset for evaluating visual question answering on document images. The LOIP project uses it specifically as a validation benchmark for the Qwen2.5-VL vision-language model, with a gate threshold of ANLS (Average Normalized Levenshtein Similarity) >= 0.75.

Visual question answering: The DocVQA paradigm maps directly to the field extraction task. Instead of asking "What is the date on this invoice?", LOIP asks "What is the PAN number on this card?" or "What is the net pay on this salary slip?". The model must locate the relevant visual region and extract the text answer.

Field extraction: The scripts/evaluate_qwen_docvqa.py script implements the ANLS evaluation pipeline. It defines QAPair objects with image_path, document_class, question, and answers (one or more acceptable ground-truth answers). The _run_qwen() function loads the Qwen2.5-VL-3B-Instruct model and processes each QA pair by combining the document image with the question prompt.

Confidence validation: ANLS provides a confidence-calibrated metric: the normalized_similarity() function computes 1.0 minus the Levenshtein distance divided by the maximum length, then clamps anything below 0.5 to 0.0. This means near-misses (OCR errors like "O" vs "0") still receive partial credit, while completely wrong answers are zeroed out. The ANLS_GATE_THRESHOLD of 0.75 is imported from domains/document_intel/schemas.py.

Current status: DEFERRED. The source (rrc.cvc.uab.es) requires a registered account/login for direct download. The ANLS scoring math is unit-tested with synthetic QA pairs (tests/models/test_evaluate_qwen_docvqa.py). The live evaluation gate is deferred to the production phase when weights and a held-out QA set become available.

Limitations:

  • Requires account registration at rrc.cvc.uab.es.
  • Approximately 3 GB compressed.
  • The Qwen2.5-VL model requires .venv-ml with transformers/torch/Pillow, which is not installed by default.

Layer 3: Tabular Training Data (generate_synthetic_dataset.py)

Generates four CSV datasets for the tabular/graph ML models used in scoring and decision-making. Each uses heuristic calibration formulas (explicitly documented as not derived from real default/fraud outcomes):

income_synthetic.csv (1000 rows, 4 columns):

  • Columns: salary_slip_amount, bank_credit_amount, anomalies, label
  • Purpose: Train XGBoostWrapper for income confidence scoring
  • Generation logic: True income from random_salary_components(tier)["net_pay"]. Clean cases add Gaussian noise (sigma=0.02). income_mismatch/income_inflated presets inflate salary slip by 1.3-1.8x. Anomaly count incremented when relative discrepancy > 0.3 or when bank_credit_amount is 0.
  • Label formula: clip(1.0 - mean_relative_discrepancy - 0.1 * anomalies + N(0, 0.02))
  • Tamper distribution: 55% clean, 10% income_mismatch, 10% income_inflated, 5% each for identity/dob/employer/pan mismatch, 2.5% each for document_forgery/synthetic_identity, 5% missing_fields

risk_synthetic.csv (1000 rows, 8 columns):

  • Columns: identity_confidence, income_confidence, foir, cibil_score_normalized, cashflow_stability, employment_tier, loan_to_income_ratio, label
  • Purpose: Train XGBoostWrapper for composite risk/approval-likelihood scoring
  • Generation logic: Features sampled from Beta distributions (identity_confidence: Beta(8,2), foir: Beta(2,3)*1.3 capped at 1.0, etc.)
  • Label formula: 0.25*identity_conf + 0.25*income_conf + 0.20*(1-foir) + 0.15*cibil_norm + 0.10*cashflow + 0.05*(1-(tier-1)/4) - 0.15*min(lti/5,1) + N(0,0.03), clipped to [0,1]
  • Decision thresholds: score >= 0.70 = APPROVE, 0.40-0.70 = REVIEW, < 0.40 = REJECT

affordability_synthetic.csv (1000 rows, 4 columns):

  • Columns: foir, disposable_income, liquidity_score, label
  • Purpose: Train LightGBMWrapper for affordability assessment
  • Note: The live processor currently hardcodes liquidity_score=0.8 (not yet computed from bank statements). This dataset varies liquidity_score in [0,1] as a forward-looking calibration target.
  • Label formula: 0.5*(1-foir) + 0.3*liquidity + 0.2*clip(disposable_income/50000) + N(0,0.03)

fraud_synthetic.csv (1000 rows, 4 columns):

  • Columns: pan_match_count, aadhaar_match_count, total_degree, label
  • Purpose: Train GraphSAGEWrapper for synthetic identity ring detection
  • Generation logic: Creates a population of 1000 applications, each with unique PAN and Aadhaar. ~8% (ring_fraction) are grouped into 2-3 member "rings" sharing a PAN or Aadhaar value. Features count the number of other applications sharing identifiers.
  • Label: Binary (1 if total_degree > 0, else 0)

Annotation Generation Pipeline (scripts/annotate/generate_annotations.py)

The pipeline takes synthetic document images + their ground-truth JSON metadata and produces two annotation formats:

  1. LayoutLMv3 annotation (FUNSD-format): Each OCR-detected word token gets a normalized bounding box (0-1000 scale) and a BIO label. Output includes token list, label schema, matched fields, and match rate.
  1. Donut annotation: A flat gt_parse dictionary mapping field names to string values for sequence-to-sequence training.

Processing steps:

  1. Load document image with Pillow
  2. Run PaddleOCR (paddleocr with use_textline_orientation=True, lang="en")
  3. Filter OCR results with confidence < 0.5
  4. Split line-level OCR results into word tokens with proportionally estimated bounding boxes
  5. Build ground-truth lookup from metadata using the label schema
  6. Match OCR tokens to ground-truth values using fuzzy matching (Levenshtein distance <= 2 for non-exact fields)
  7. For multi-word fields, use sliding window matching over consecutive tokens
  8. Assign BIO labels: first matched token gets B-TAG, subsequent tokens get I-TAG

Fuzzy matching logic: The _fuzzy_match() function strips commas, currency symbols, and colons, converts to uppercase, and compares. For exact fields (identifiers), strict equality is required. For other fields, Levenshtein distance <= 2 is accepted. For very short ground-truth values (< 3 characters), exact match is required.

Annotation Validation (scripts/annotate/validate_annotations.py)

The validation script checks that every expected ground-truth field has at least one annotated token with the correct BIO label. It compares the assigned BIO tags in the LayoutLMv3 annotation against expected fields derived from the metadata and label schema. Default minimum match rate threshold is 80%.

The validator checks:

  • All schema-defined fields that have non-empty values in metadata
  • Aadhaar address fields (if any address component is non-empty)
  • Bank statement transaction fields (if transactions exist)

Output per document: pass/fail status, match rate, covered fields, and missing fields.

7.7 Data Augmentation Techniques

The project does NOT use traditional image augmentation (rotation, flipping, color jitter, noise injection, etc.). Instead, it achieves data diversity through:

  1. Randomized document content: Every generated document has randomized field values (names, PAN numbers, addresses, salary components, dates) using Faker("en_IN") and custom random generators.
  1. Employer tier-based salary variation: 5 tiers with different base salary ranges and weighted probability distribution (tier 3 = 30%, tier 2 = 25%, tier 5 = 15%).
  1. Tamper type variation: The corpus generation script (generate_corpus.py) distributes tampered documents evenly across supported tamper types using _tamper_sequence().
  1. Bank statement transaction diversity: Each statement generates 6 months of transactions with 8-20 random expenses per month, 0-2 random EMIs, and occasional miscellaneous credits (30% probability). Expense narrations vary across 15 categories (UPI-SWIGGY, ATM WITHDRAWAL, etc.).
  1. Scale factor: generate_corpus.py supports a --scale parameter (default 1.0) for producing smaller smoke-test batches (e.g., --scale 0.1 = 1,050 documents).

SECTION 8

- DOCUMENT UNDERSTANDING PIPELINE

The pipeline is orchestrated by DocumentIntelligenceProcessor (loip/domains/document_intel/processor.py). It accepts a numpy BGR image and returns {classification, ocr, extraction, qr}.

Stage 0 -- Preprocessing (loip/models/preprocessing.py)

5-step pipeline: deskew (Hough lines, +/-15 deg cap) -> deblur (Laplacian unsharp mask) -> adaptive threshold (Sauvola-style, OFF by default -- destroys color for LayoutLMv3) -> denoise (bilateral filter) -> border crop (2% trim). Each step independently toggleable. _require_cv2() raises ImportError if OpenCV missing.

Stage 1 -- Classification (processor.py:102-118)

  • Primary: LayoutLMv3Wrapper.classify() runs PaddleOCR internally for word+bbox extraction, normalizes to 0-1000 coords, feeds through LayoutLMv3ForSequenceClassification (6 classes: AADHAAR, BANK_STATEMENT, FORM16, ITR, PAN, SALARY_SLIP). Softmax over logits.
  • Confidence gate: < 0.85 sets needs_review=True.
  • Fallback: In real mode with low confidence, _classify_via_ollama() sends base64 PNG to Qwen2.5-VL via Ollama /api/generate, asks for one of 9 document types. Partial-match fallback on unrecognized responses. Network errors (OSError/ValueError) caught, returns None.

Stage 2 -- OCR (processor.py:120-135)

  • Primary: PaddleOCRWrapper (angle classification, English). Polygon corners converted to axis-aligned (x0,y0,x1,y1).
  • Fallback: If mean_confidence < 0.9, invokes SuryaOCRWrapper (currently stub).
  • Conflict detection: If confidence delta > 0.15 between engines, returns OCRConflict with both results for human review. Otherwise returns higher-confidence result.

Stage 3 -- Field Extraction (processor.py:152-159)

  • Primary: Qwen25VLWrapper.extract_fields() -- constructs prompt with FIELD_SPECS[doc_class] field names, sends to Qwen2.5-VL (Ollama preferred, HF transformers fallback). Parses first JSON object from response via regex. Confidence: 0.90 (all fields) or 0.70 (partial).
  • Fallback: If overall_confidence < 0.7, invokes DonutWrapper.extract_structured() (VisionEncoderDecoder with custom task token). Returns whichever has higher confidence.
  • FIELD_SPECS: 9 document types with 4-12 fields each (e.g., salary_slip has 12 fields including employer_name, net_pay, pf_deduction, tds_deduction).

Stage 4 -- QR Detection (processor.py:137-150)

Only for PAN and AADHAAR. Chain: pyzbar (confidence=1.0) -> OpenCV QRCodeDetector (confidence=0.85). QRTrustProcessor lazily imported to avoid circular imports.

Stage 5 -- Caching & Assembly (processor.py:161-188)

MD5 hash of image array as cache key (falls back to id(image) on hash failure). In-memory dict cache. Returns {classification, ocr, extraction, qr}.

Traceability (EvidenceChain) (loip/schemas/evidence.py)

Every decision carries an EvidenceChain: auto-UUID chain_id, human-readable claim, list of ExtractedField (with SourceLocation containing document_id, page, BoundingBox 0-1000, extraction_method enum, model_version), contradicting fields, reconciled_value, reconciliation_method (SOURCE_TRUST_WEIGHTED / MAJORITY_VOTE / HIGHEST_CONFIDENCE / API_AUTHORITATIVE / COMPUTED), confidence, timestamp.

SECTION 13

- TAMPERING DETECTION

ELA (tampering.py:69-84): Re-save at JPEG quality=75, compute mean(|original - reloaded|)/255.0. Threshold 0.15.

EXIF (tampering.py:89-139): Checks Software/ProcessingSoftware/ImageDescription for editing keywords (photoshop, gimp, illustrator, lightroom, affinity, pixelmator, acrobat, inkscape). Also flags GPS stripping, DateTime vs DateTimeOriginal discrepancy, dimension mismatch (EXIF vs actual >2px).

Combined rule: overall_tampered = ela_anomaly AND exif_anomaly -- requires both signals to prevent false positives from noisy scans.

Aadhaar QR RSA signature (aadhaar_qr.py:92-150): UIDAI Secure QR = zlib_xml || 256-byte RSA-2048 PKCS1v15+SHA256 signature. Uses cryptography library. Placeholder keys detected and logged. All failures return False (never raise).

PAN QR format (pan_qr.py): Regex ^[A-Z]{5}[0-9]{4}[A-Z]$. Supports Base64 pipe-delimited and plain-text formats.

PDF metadata (identity_trust/processor.py:121-126): Checks producer/creator for "photoshop" -> DOCUMENT_METADATA_ANOMALY flag.

Cross-field (QR vs OCR) (qr_trust/processor.py:253-340): SequenceMatcher for name/address, exact match for DOB/UID-last4/PAN. Flags: QR_NAME_MISMATCH, QR_DOB_MISMATCH, QR_ADDRESS_MISMATCH, QR_ID_NUMBER_MISMATCH.

Fraud pipeline (fraud/processor.py): 4 rules -- (1) liveness spoof (severity 0.9), (2) MRZ ICAO 9303 checksum (severity 0.9), (3) Neo4j identity-graph rings (best-effort, exception-safe), (4) GraphSAGE anomaly (>0.8 = SYNTHETIC_IDENTITY_RING). Final fraud_score = max(severities). Each signal carries an EvidenceChain.

Trust score (qr_trust/processor.py:346-378): 1.0 minus deductions: QR_NOT_FOUND(-0.20), QR_DECODE_FAILED(-0.20), QR_SIGNATURE_INVALID(-0.35), QR_SIGNATURE_MISSING(-0.10), each failed field match(-0.10), overall_tampered(-0.30), ela_anomaly-only(-0.10), exif_anomaly-only(-0.10). Floor 0.0.

File: loip/models/preprocessing.py

Type: Classical computer vision pipeline (no learned parameters).

5-Stage Pipeline:

  1. Deskew: Hough line detection to find dominant line angles, then affine rotation to correct skew up to +/-15 degrees. Median angle calculation with 0.3-degree dead zone.

  5. Border Crop: Removes 2% of image from each edge to eliminate scan borders.

17.1 EvidenceChain (loip/schemas/evidence.py)

The traceability contract is defined in loip/schemas/evidence.py and enforced across all domain outputs. The core data structures are:

EvidenceChain (Pydantic BaseModel):

  • chain_id: Auto-generated UUID v4 per chain instance.
  • claim: Human-readable assertion (e.g., "applicant_annual_income = 12,00,000").
  • supporting: List of ExtractedField objects that back the claim.
  • contradicting: List of ExtractedField objects that conflict with the claim (default empty).
  • reconciled_value: The final resolved value (str or float) after multi-source reconciliation.
  • reconciliation_method: One of ReconciliationMethod enum: SOURCE_TRUST_WEIGHTED, MAJORITY_VOTE, HIGHEST_CONFIDENCE, API_AUTHORITATIVE, COMPUTED.
  • confidence: Float [0.0, 1.0] representing trust in the reconciled value.
  • created_at: UTC timestamp.

SourceLocation (Pydantic BaseModel):

  • document_id: UUID linking to the stored document in MinIO object storage.
  • document_type: DocumentType enum covering PAN, Aadhaar, salary_slip, bank_statement, form16, ITR, GST_return, offer_letter, passport, driving_licence.
  • is_synthetic: Boolean flag for generated test data (critical for audit trail integrity).
  • page_number: Optional page within a multi-page document.
  • coordinates: Optional BoundingBox (normalized 0-1000, LayoutLMv3 convention) pinpointing the exact region on the document page.
  • extraction_method: ExtractionMethod enum covering PaddleOCR, Surya, Qwen2.5-VL, Donut, UIDAI API, NSDL API, CIBIL API, Experian API, DigiLocker, human_entry, QR_decode.
  • model_version: Explicit model version string (e.g., "paddleocr-4.0", "qwen2.5-vl-7b-v1").

ExtractedField (Pydantic BaseModel):

  • field_name: Semantic field name (e.g., "pan_number", "annual_income", "employer_name").
  • raw_value: The original extracted string before normalization.
  • normalized_value: Post-normalization value (optional).
  • confidence: Float [0.0, 1.0] from the extraction model.
  • source: A SourceLocation providing full provenance.
  • verified_by: List of verification model IDs or API names that confirmed this field.

18.6 Data Encryption

At Rest (backend/src/utils/crypto.ts):

  • Algorithm: AES-256-CBC.
  • Key: 32-byte key loaded from ENCRYPTION_KEY environment variable (64 hex chars). If missing/invalid, a temporary random key is generated per process with a console warning -- this means encrypted data would be unrecoverable after restart.

  • encryptBuffer(data): Returns IV || ciphertext.
  • decryptBuffer(encryptedWithIv): Extracts IV from first 16 bytes, decrypts remainder.

In Transit:

  • No explicit TLS configuration found in the codebase. This would be handled at the infrastructure/reverse-proxy level (nginx, load balancer).

Gaps identified:

SECTION 15

- AFFORDABILITY ENGINE

Python Backend (loip/domains/affordability/processor.py)

Income Reconstruction:

  • Verified monthly income is passed in from the IncomeIntelligenceProcessor result, not reconstructed within affordability itself.
  • The IncomeIntelligenceProcessor (loip/domains/income_intel/processor.py) performs multi-source income reconstruction using source-trust weighting across: salary slips (weight 0.65), bank statement credits (weight 0.65-0.75 depending on segment), ITR current FY (weight 0.85-0.90), ITR prior FY (weight 0.80), and GST returns (weight 0.75).
  • Self-employed applicants get different weights: ITR at 0.90 (highest), bank at 0.65, GST turnover * 25% profit margin.
  • Reconciled annual income = sum(annual_amount * trust_weight) / sum(trust_weight).

FOIR Calculation:

  • FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) = total_obligations / verified_monthly_income.
  • total_obligations = existing_obligations (from bank statement, currently hardcoded to 0.0 -- NOT IMPLEMENTED for existing EMI parsing) + proposed_emi.
  • EMI computed using standard reducing-balance formula: P * r * (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), default interest rate 14% p.a.
  • FOIR is clamped to [0.0, 1.0].

DTI Calculation:

  • DTI is set equal to FOIR (dti = min(foir, 1.0)) -- they are treated as equivalent in the current implementation.

Expense Estimation:

  • Hardcoded at INR 15,000/month (estimated_monthly_expenses = 15000.0).
  • Disposable income = verified_monthly_income - total_obligations - 15,000.

Affordability Flags:

  • FOIR_EXCEEDED: FOIR > 0.60 (hard reject threshold).
  • FOIR_MARGINAL: FOIR > 0.50 (review trigger).
  • DISPOSABLE_INCOME_INSUFFICIENT: disposable_income < 0.

Overall Project Assessment

LOIP is an architecturally ambitious and technically sophisticated loan onboarding intelligence platform that demonstrates deep domain expertise in Indian regulatory fintech. The codebase spans approximately 14,300 lines of Python across 132 files, supplemented by a React/Express analytics portal, with 12 Docker-composed infrastructure services, 11 ML model wrappers, 13 domain processors, and 111 test functions. The system addresses a genuine market need -- automated personal loan underwriting compliant with RBI Digital Lending Guidelines and the DPDP Act 2023.

The project is at an advanced prototype stage (62% overall completion per the project's own assessment, which I find accurate). It successfully demonstrates the full conceptual pipeline from document upload through fraud detection to explainable decisioning, but operates primarily in mock mode with deterministic responses rather than trained production models.

Strengths

1. Domain Architecture Excellence

The 13-domain processor architecture (document intelligence, identity trust, income intelligence, affordability, fraud, risk decisioning, explainability, human review, compliance, truth reconciliation, QR trust, V-CIP, MLOps) represents a mature understanding of loan origination complexity. Each domain is cleanly separated with its own processor, schemas, and evidence chains. The OnboardingPipeline orchestrator (onboarding.py, 181 LOC) is remarkably clean for the complexity it manages.

2. Evidence Traceability

Every decision field traces back to a source document through EvidenceChain objects containing ExtractedField entries linked to SourceLocation with document_id, extraction_method, and model_version. This is a regulatory requirement that many platforms implement as an afterthought; LOIP builds it into every processor from the ground up.

3. Regulatory Awareness

DPDP consent management, PII masking (Presidio + regex fallback), KFS generation with APR calculation, cooling-off period enforcement, NACH mandate handling, PEP/AML screening, V-CIP lifecycle, data residency checks, and Aadhaar QR RSA-2048 signature verification demonstrate thorough regulatory knowledge. The ComplianceProcessor (359 LOC) is the most complete compliance implementation I have seen in a platform at this stage.

4. QR Trust Verification Module

The Aadhaar Secure QR (zlib decompress, XML parse, RSA-2048 PKCS#1 v1.5 + SHA-256 signature verification) and PAN QR (Base64/pipe-delimited parse) with ELA + EXIF tampering detection is a genuinely novel capability that adds cryptographic document verification beyond what most loan platforms attempt.

5. Dual-Mode Pipeline Design

The mock_mode toggle throughout every model wrapper and processor means the system can run end-to-end without any ML weights, external APIs, or Docker services. This is invaluable for development, testing, CI, and demos. The real mode for document intelligence (verified working with Ollama + Qwen2.5-VL) demonstrates the upgrade path works.

6. Human Review Workflow

The ReviewProcessor implements the full case management lifecycle: creation from decisions, queue filtering/sorting/pagination, case assignment, override workflow (approve/reject/escalate), feature snapshots for retraining data, and queue summary statistics. This is production-adjacent quality.

7. Webcam Liveness Detection

InsightFace buffalo_l powering a 3-step challenge (head turn right/left + blink detection) using 2D-106 landmark EAR calculation is a working biometric gate that adds genuine security to the demo, not just a mockup.

Weaknesses

1. No Trained Production Models

This is the single most critical gap. All 11 ML models operate in mock mode. The XGBoost risk scorer returns 0.75 for every input. The GraphSAGE fraud detector returns 0.15 for every input. The LayoutLMv3 classifier returns deterministic document classes. Without trained models, the platform cannot make real decisions.

2. Dual Backend Architecture Without Integration

The Python/FastAPI backend and Express/Node.js backend are two independent systems with different databases (PostgreSQL/JSON vs SQLite/PostgreSQL), different processing engines (13 Python processors vs 14 TypeScript engines), different authentication systems (permission-based vs JWT), and no shared state. A loan processed through one path is invisible to the other. This architectural split doubles the maintenance burden and creates confusion about which system is authoritative.

3. Express Backend Security Issues

  • Encryption key fallback (crypto.ts lines 10-16) generates random keys on missing ENCRYPTION_KEY, making encrypted documents unrecoverable after restart
  • No input validation on sandboxCibil, sandboxDebt, sandboxUtil parameters allows arbitrary credit score injection
  • Mock data fallback in getLoanById (line 167-169) means database errors silently return fake data to users
  • Document deletion (deleteDocument) permanently removes encrypted files from disk without soft-delete or backup

4. Linting and Security Debt

362 ruff findings and 95 bandit findings (including 1 High severity) run as continue-on-error in CI. This means CI never fails on code quality or security issues. The BLE001 (bare except) noqa comments appear 20+ times across the codebase.

5. Affordability Model Limitations

Existing obligations hardcoded to 0.0, monthly expenses hardcoded to INR 15,000, liquidity score hardcoded to 0.8, cashflow stability hardcoded to 0.8, financial stress score hardcoded to 0.1. The affordability assessment is therefore an EMI-to-income ratio calculator, not a genuine affordability model.

  • No production API endpoints for NSDL/UIDAI/CIBIL

Executive Recommendation

LOIP is a technically impressive proof-of-concept that demonstrates the full complexity of AI-driven loan origination for the Indian market. The architecture is sound, the domain model is comprehensive, and the regulatory awareness is above average for an early-stage platform.

The critical path to value is: train real models, unify the backends, and deploy to a cloud environment. The project should consolidate on the Python/FastAPI backend as the primary system (it has the more sophisticated domain model with evidence chains, explainability, and regulatory compliance), relegate the Express backend to a BFF (backend-for-frontend) role for the React portal, and invest heavily in building a labeled Indian document corpus for model training.

With 3-4 months of focused engineering effort and a team of 3-4 engineers, LOIP could reach an MVP deployable for a pilot with one NBFC partner. The biggest risk is not technical but data-related: without a substantial corpus of labeled Indian financial documents and labeled loan outcomes, the ML models cannot be trained to production accuracy.

Verdict: Strong prototype, clear vision, solid architecture, premature for production. The gap between "working demo" and "production-ready platform" is approximately 6 months of full-time engineering, $200K-400K in compute/data costs, and one pilot NBFC partner willing to provide labeled training data.
